## 1. Event Visualization Philosophy

Events are the narrative backbone of the system.

Event visualization must:

- communicate chronology
- communicate causality
- communicate confidence
- communicate reversibility
- communicate operational impact

---

## 2. Event Categories

### Biological Events

Examples:

- birth
- insemination
- pregnancy confirmation
- illness
- treatment
- death

Visual characteristics:

- organic colors
- biological iconography
- timeline prominence

---

### Operational Events

Examples:

- movement
- relocation
- grouping
- weighing
- inventory operations

Visual characteristics:

- structured layout
- neutral operational colors
- high readability

---

### Financial Events

Examples:

- purchase
- sale
- invoice
- compensation

Visual characteristics:

- accounting clarity
- stronger structure
- transactional tone

---

### System Events

Examples:

- automated recalculation
- synchronization
- generated alerts

Visual characteristics:

- visually secondary
- subtle styling
- clearly system-generated

---

## 3. Event States

### Confirmed Event

Characteristics:

- solid
- high contrast
- permanent
- visually anchored

---

### Pending Event

Characteristics:

- lighter tone
- reduced emphasis
- provisional feel

---

### Corrected Event

Characteristics:

- preserved in history
- linked to compensation event
- never hidden

---

### Critical Event

Characteristics:

- strong contrast
- alert treatment
- contextual explanation

---

## 4. Timeline Rules

Timelines should:

- feel chronological
- emphasize causality
- avoid visual clutter
- preserve operational scanning

Timelines must never:

- hide timestamps
- flatten event hierarchy
- treat all events equally

Important events should visually dominate.
