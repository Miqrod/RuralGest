## 1. Forms Represent Intent

Forms are NOT database editors.

Forms represent:

- operational intention
- domain workflows
- guided actions

The user should feel:

- assisted
- guided
- protected

Never overwhelmed.

---

## 2. Form Design Principles

### Progressive Disclosure

Do not show everything immediately.

Reveal:

- contextual sections
- dependent inputs
- advanced configuration

only when relevant.

---

### Context Before Confirmation

Before saving:

- summarize consequences
- summarize affected entities
- explain generated actions if necessary

---

### Biological Clarity

Biological information should:

- feel distinct from administrative data
- feel organic and operational
- preserve readability

---

### Operational Safety

Dangerous actions require:

- confirmation
- contextual awareness
- visibility of impact

---

## 3. Form Layout Rules

Forms should:

- use clear section grouping
- separate biological and administrative concepts
- preserve scanning rhythm
- avoid visual fatigue

Avoid:

- giant uninterrupted forms
- overly dense tables inside forms
- excessive nesting
